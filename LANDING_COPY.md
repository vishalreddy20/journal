## FAQ Section

**Q: Is this actually free?**  
A: Yes. No credit card, no trial, no "free tier" with hidden limits. The audit is free because it's valuable to us when you share it — every shared result URL is a distribution event for Credex.

**Q: Where does the pricing data come from?**  
A: Every number cites an official vendor pricing page with a verification date. We update pricing weekly. If you find an error, email us and we'll correct it within 24 hours.

**Q: Do you save my company's spend data?**  
A: Audit results are saved to generate your shareable link. Your email is only stored if you choose to save your report — it's never required. We never sell data to third parties.

**Q: What if I'm already spending optimally?**  
A: We'll tell you clearly. No manufactured savings — "You're spending well 🎉" is a real output. We'll notify you if new optimizations become available as plans change.

**Q: What's Credex and how does this relate?**  
A: SpendLens is built by Credex, a marketplace for discounted AI infrastructure credits. Companies that overforecast their AI usage sell unused credits through Credex at below-retail rates. If the audit finds significant savings, we'll show you how Credex can help capture more. If not, we won't bother you.

**Q: How long does the audit take?**  
A: 2 minutes. The form has 3 short steps. No signup required.

## Copy Principles

1. **CFO-ready framing**: Annual savings > monthly savings as the top-line number. Finance people think in annual figures.
2. **Specificity over generality**: "$840/month" beats "significant savings." Every number is real.
3. **No dark patterns**: The free audit is genuinely free. The email is optional, not required. The copy says this explicitly.
4. **Source trust signals**: "Official vendor pricing" and "sources cited" appear near the audit results — because user interview 3 confirmed distrust of black-box financial advice.
5. **Brevity**: The 2-minute claim is real and the form is designed to honor it. The copy makes this promise explicitly because it's the #1 objection ("I don't want to spend 20 minutes filling out a form" — R.M., Interview 2).
